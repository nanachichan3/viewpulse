## Outreach Strategy

Three-tier approach based on channel size and engagement likelihood:

### Tier 1 — Mega Reach (1M+, hardest to get)
These won't respond to cold DMs. Require either:
- Mutual connection
- Compelling hook + value exchange
- Or skip to Tier 2-3 for actual launch traction

### Tier 2 — Mid-tier creators (50K–1M, best ROI)
These actively look for interesting tools to showcase. Have established review/discovery content. Respond to genuine DMs + emails.

### Tier 3 — Micro-creators (10K–50K, highest conversion)
Most responsive. Often do genuine tool reviews. Willing to try new tools for content. Lower production friction for them to make a video.

## Outreach Message Templates

### Twitter DM (Tier 2 — Mid-tier creator)
```
Hey [Name]! Big fan of your content — especially the [specific video/post].

I recently built Viewpulse (viewpulse.rachkovan.com), a YouTube analytics tool for creators. YouTube shows you views. Viewpulse shows you WHY — retention curves, traffic sources, optimal posting windows.

Would love to get your honest feedback. No strings attached, happy to credit you if you ever want to share with your audience.
```

### Twitter DM (Tier 3 — Micro-creator)
```
Hi [Name]! I love what you're doing with [niche] content.

I built Viewpulse — a new YouTube analytics tool. Upload your watch history, play with your data. It's free during launch.

Would be amazing if you'd check it out and let me know what you think. Always looking for genuine feedback from creators like you 🙏
```

### Reply-guy approach (Tier 1 — Mega)
1. Find their tweet about analytics, views, YouTube Studio
2. Reply with genuine insight + mention Viewpulse naturally
3. Don't ask for anything — just add value
4. Repeat 3-5 times over 2 weeks before any soft pitch
