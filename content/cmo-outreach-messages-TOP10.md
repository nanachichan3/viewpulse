## Follow-Up Response Templates

### When a creator DMs to say they tried it:
> Thanks for giving it a go, [NAME]. I'd love to know what surprised you or what felt missing — I built this for myself first and genuinely iterate based on creator feedback.
>
> Any features you'd want added? I'm working on [upcoming feature] next.

### When a creator asks for a feature:
> Great question — that's actually on my list. [What they asked about]. Not sure if it'll make the next sprint, but it's exactly the kind of feedback I need.
>
> Want me to ping you when it's live? And if you ever want to chat more about your analytics approach, I'm always up for it.

### When a creator offers to cover/review it:
> That would be amazing — I'd genuinely love that. [Link to dashboard] is the best way to get a feel for it. Happy to jump on a call or share any assets you need for a review.
>
> No rush, and I understand if it doesn't fit your content calendar. Just let me know either way — and thanks for even reaching out, that means a lot.

### When a creator gives critical feedback:
> This is exactly what I needed to hear, thank you. [Specific feedback point] — you're right, that's a real gap. I [will fix it / am thinking about how to approach it].
>
> Would you be open to me following up with you once I've addressed it? Genuinely want to build something creators find useful, not just something technically impressive.

### When a creator asks "how does this compare to TubeBuddy/VidIQ?":
> Good question. The big difference is philosophy — Viewpulse doesn't collect your data. No accounts, no tracking, your analytics stay yours. That means it won't do channel comparisons or "discover what your audience wants" — it's focused purely on giving you clear answers from your own data.
>
> I'd say: TubeBuddy/VidIQ if you want discovery features and don't mind data sharing. Viewpulse if you want a clean, privacy-first look at your own numbers without the noise.
>
> Happy to answer any other comparison questions.
