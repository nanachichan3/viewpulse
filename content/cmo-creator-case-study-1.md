## The Problem TechTutorMike Had

Mike was staring at his YouTube Studio dashboard every morning and couldn't answer the simplest question:

> *"Why did my 'How to use Notion' video get 40,000 views while my 'How to use Obsidian' video — posted the same day, same length, same thumbnail style — got only 9,000?"*

YouTube Studio showed him the views. It couldn't show him why.

He tried exporting CSVs, building spreadsheets, manually cross-referencing upload dates with traffic sources. It was a mess. He needed a clearer picture.

---

```
📊 YouTube Studio — TechTutorMike (Last 28 Days)
```

**What this tells Mike:** His Notion video did well. He should make more Notion content.

**What this DOESN'T tell Mike:** Is that actually the right conclusion?

---

## What Viewpulse Revealed (The Full Story)

### Insight 1: CTR by Traffic Source — The Thumbnail Wasn't the Problem

| Video | Total CTR | Search CTR | Suggested CTR | Browse CTR |
|-------|-----------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| How to use Notion | 8.2% | **14.1%** | 3.1% | 5.8% |
| How to use Obsidian | 4.7% | 6.3% | 2.8% | 4.2% |

**Viewpulse insight:** TechTutorMike's Notion video crushed it on **search CTR** (14.1%) — his title and thumbnail were perfect for search intent. The Obsidian video underperformed because the topic had lower overall search volume, not because of the content quality.

**Actionable takeaway:** Mike shouldn't necessarily make more Notion content — he should make more content where he has high search intent competition. Same approach, better targeting.

---

### Insight 2: Avg. View Duration by Video Type — The "Short" Videos Are Hurting Him

| Video type | Avg. length | Avg. retention | Avg. watch time |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Full tutorials | 18–22 min | 52% | 9–11 min |
| Quick tips | 5–8 min | 41% | 2–3 min |
| Tool reviews | 10–14 min | 58% | 6–8 min |

**Viewpulse insight:** His "quick tips" videos have surprisingly low retention (41%) despite being short. People click expecting a quick answer but the video doesn't deliver fast enough. His 18–22 min tutorials actually perform best by watch time.

**Actionable takeaway:** Either shorten the quick tips significantly (< 4 min) or restructure them to answer the question faster. His audience wants depth or speed — not medium-length filler.

---

### Insight 3: Subscriber Delta Per Video — The "Viral" Video Wasn't Growing His Channel

| Video | Views | New subs | Unsubs | Net delta |
|-------|-------|----------|--------|----------|
| How to use Notion | 40,200 | +89 | -31 | **+58** |
| VS Code Tips | 31,500 | +142 | -22 | **+120** |
| GitHub Copilot Review | 28,100 | +67 | -18 | **+49** |
| Quick tip: Terminal | 12,400 | +18 | -24 | **-6** |

**Viewpulse insight:** His "Quick tip: Terminal" video actually lost subscribers (-6 net). High views, low retention, low value-per-view — YouTube pushed it to the wrong audience and they bounced.

The VS Code Tips video drove the most net subscribers despite not being the most-viewed. Why? Higher retention + audience fit.

**Actionable takeaway:** Focus on the content type that retains his *actual* audience, not whatever YouTube is pushing. Subscriber deltas per video are the real health metric.

## Takeaways for Other Creators

1. **Views ≠ growth.** High views with high unsubs = audience mismatch.
2. **Search CTR and Suggested CTR tell you different things.** High search CTR = your SEO/thumbnail is working for intent. High suggested CTR = YouTube thinks your video solves a problem it matched.
3. **Retention % without context is useless.** 50% on 10 min ≠ 50% on 20 min. Viewpulse shows the actual watch time.
4. **Subscriber delta per video is the metric YouTube doesn't show.** It tells you which videos your *real* audience actually values.
