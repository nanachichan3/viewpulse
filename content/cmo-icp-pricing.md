# Viewpulse ICP & Pricing Strategy

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Target YouTuber Profile

| Attribute | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Subscriber Range** | 10,000 – 500,000 subscribers |
| **Niche** | Tech/product reviews, education, gaming, productivity, coding/programming |
| **Upload Cadence** | Regular uploads — minimum 1 video/week |
| **Platform Reliance** | YouTube is primary or significant secondary income |
| **Pain Point** | YouTube Studio gives basic numbers but no trend context, no comparative benchmarks, no deep CTR analysis by thumbnail or title |
| **Tech Comfort** | Comfortable using web apps; OAuth sign-in is a non-issue |
| **Decision Trigger** | Wants to understand why a video underperformed or wants to optimize thumbnails/titles before publishing |
| **Privacy Sensitivity** | Values privacy; skeptical of tools that harvest their channel data |
| **Content Type** | Long-form (10–30 min), short-form (under 5 min), or mixed |

### ICP Segment Breakdown

**Segment A — Growing Tech Reviewers (Primary)**
- 50K–300K subs
- Review gadgets, software, apps, or hardware
- Upload 2–4× per week
- Revenue: AdSense + affiliate + occasional sponsored content
- Core need: Benchmark CTR against peers; understand thumbnail performance

**Segment B — Education & How-To Creators (Primary)**
- 20K–200K subs
- Tutorials, explainers, course-adjacent content
- Upload 1–3× per week
- Revenue: AdSense + courses + Patreon
- Core need: Track avg view duration as a proxy for content quality; identify drop-off points

**Segment C — Productivity & Lifestyle YouTubers (Secondary)**
- 30K–500K subs
- Productivity tips, life hacks, morning routines
- Upload 1–2× per week
- Core need: Watch time pattern analysis; understand audience retention across videos

**Segment D — Gaming Creators (Secondary)**
- 30K–400K subs
- Game reviews, gameplay commentary, walkthroughs
- Upload 3–5× per week (high volume)
- Core need: CTR analysis for thumbnails; subscriber delta tracking tied to upload schedule

### Anti-ICP (Who NOT to Target)

- **Under 10K subs**: Not enough data to generate meaningful analytics; too early in journey
- **Over 500K subs**: Likely already using premium analytics tools (TubeBuddy, vidIQ); budget is different
- **Casual/vlog channels**: Irregular uploads; analytics not a pressing need
- **YouTube Studio power users**: Already getting what they need from native analytics

## 2. Pricing Tier Structure

### Free — "Starter"

**Price:** $0/month
**Target:** Creators just hitting the 10K sub mark, or creators who want to trial before committing.

| Feature | Included |
|---------|----------|
| Channels | 1 channel |
| Dashboard | Basic metrics (views, likes, comments) |
| CTR Analysis | ❌ |
| Avg View Duration tracking | ❌ |
| Subscriber Delta | ✅ (7-day rolling) |
| Watch Time Patterns | ❌ |
| Historical Data | 7 days |
| OAuth Login | ✅ |

**Rationale:** Zero barrier to entry. Generates initial trust. Converts curious creators into habitual users who then upgrade when they feel the limits.

---

### Pro — "Growth" ⭐ Most Popular

**Price:** $15/month
**Target:** Creators who take analytics seriously and are actively optimizing content.

| Feature | Included |
|--------|----------|

| Watch Time Patterns | ✅ (retention curves) |

**Rationale:** $15/mo is the sweet spot — affordable enough for a creator earning $500+/mo from YouTube, priced to feel like a no-brainer upgrade. This is the revenue tier.

## 4. Pricing Psychology Notes

- **Anchor to YouTube Studio:** Frame Pro at $15 as "less than a coffee a week" — the implicit anchor is $0 (YouTube Studio).
- **Annual discount:** Offer 2 months free on annual ($150 billed annually vs $180 annually at full price) — pushes commitment and reduces churn.
- **Upgrade prompts:** When Free users hit a data limit, surface a contextual upgrade card showing exactly what's unlocked. Don't interrupt their workflow.
- **Privacy as value:** Emphasize in all tiers that their channel data is never collected, stored, or shared — this is a core differentiator to call out in pricing page copy.
